Time-synchronized multimodal acquisition

All sensor streams are acquired through a ROS-based DAQ system, providing precise time synchronization across all modalities — a critical feature for multimodal fusion algorithms, and a point of strong community interest.

Radiological + Calibration continuous
  • List-mode Digibase data — raw spectral events
  • Online calibration — continuous gain and energy tracking
  • Bin-mode spectra at 0.5 s integration time
  • ~2.5 GB/node/day (combined with meteorological)
Meteorological continuous
  • T, p, H — temperature, pressure, humidity at /30 s
  • Rain gauge — precipitation at /1 s
  • Critical for correlating rad background changes
  • ~2.5 GB/node/day (combined with radiological)
Video Camera 6×2 min/day

  • Selected acquisition synchronized with LiDAR and rad
  • ~6 GB/node/day

  • ~6 GB/node/day

Automated labeling

Three complementary label types

☢️

Radiological Labels

  • Bin-mode spectra at 0.5 s integration (timestamp, count rate, livetime)
  • Continuous energy calibration (gain, saturation, resolution; <1% stability)
  • Meteorological context (T, p, H, rain, radon flag)
  • NMF-based background model (ab-initio, in-situ learning)
  • Anomaly detection metrics (metric, p-value, GOF, NMF weights)
  • Isotope identification (top ID, encounter classification)
  • Background model index & selection history
Generated by LBNL's BARD (Background Adaptive Radiation Detection) · Default: FPR = 1/8h
📹

Camera Labels

  • Frame-by-frame bounding boxes (x, y, w, h, confidence)
  • Object classes: Bicycle, Bus, Car, Motorcycle, Pedestrian, Truck
  • YOLOv10l at 25% default confidence threshold
  • Frame-to-frame track IDs via Norfair tracker
  • Per-track metadata over 2-min footage windows
Supports detection/tracking algorithm training and rad/contextual fusion research
🔗

Rad + Camera Labels

  • Source–object attribution: assigns R/N signal to scene objects
  • Monocular depth estimation (UniK3D) for 3D track reconstruction
  • Remote extrinsic camera calibration workflow
  • Track-based modeled signal vs measured source signal fit
  • Attribution metric: p-value of chi² NNLS fit
Validated with controlled drive-by experiments at LBNL (200 µCi Co-60 source)

Why this data is unique

PANDAWN data sets are unique in the nonproliferation mission space: large-volume, synchronized, labeled, and collected from real urban environments over long time periods — capturing seasonal, meteorological, and temporal variation that cannot be replicated in simulation.

Labeling follows an iterative approach, informed by ongoing community feedback through the Detecting Radiation Algorithms Group (DRAG) and data user analyses. Current labeling represents a first pass — further refinement continues as community use cases develop.
