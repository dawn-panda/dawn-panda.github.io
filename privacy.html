Privacy Guardrails

Data collection follows strict privacy, compliance, and data-use policies

PANDAWN data collection is governed by the City of Chicago agreement and associated privacy and compliance requirements. The project is designed to collect only the data needed for the scientific mission while limiting collection of personally identifiable information and restricting access to approved research users.

No audio, genomic, biological, or PII collection

In accordance with Illinois wiretapping laws, audio data is not collected. The system does not collect genomic or biological data, and no personally identifiable information is required for the science or intentionally collected by the project.

Camera field-of-view limitations

Cameras are focused on defined areas of interest, such as intersections. Due to the angle of view and deployment configuration, the resulting imagery is insufficient to recognize people’s faces or license plates. This configuration was reviewed and accepted by the Central Department of Energy Institutional Review Board.

Limited video and LiDAR collection

Under the current agreements, LiDAR and video data are collected only six times per day for two minutes per collection window for training and evaluation purposes. This represents less than 1% of the day, while radiological and environmental sensor streams are collected continuously.

Restricted data access

Data access is initially restricted to approved members of the research teams at ANL, PNNL, and LBNL. Individuals must sign a data-use agreement and comply with its terms and conditions; access may be revoked if those requirements are not followed.

Communication Plan

Standard reporting process for anomalous radiological measurements

The PANDAWN Research Platform includes a communication plan that defines a Standard Operating Procedure for reporting anomalous radiological measurements observed during routine analysis of data recorded by research activities and experiments in Chicago.

When anomalies are observed

If anomalous radiological measurements are identified during routine PANDAWN data analysis, the project follows the established communication plan and reporting procedure. This ensures that observations are handled consistently, responsibly, and through the appropriate channels.

Standard Operating Procedure

The communication plan defines a Standard Operating Procedure for documenting, evaluating, and reporting anomalous measurements associated with PANDAWN research data and experiments conducted in Chicago.

Agency notification

Under this plan, the appropriate agencies are notified through the standard process followed by the Radiological Assistance Program, Region 5.

DOE Human Subjects Research

DOE compliance review and data assurance documentation

The PANDAWN project and its research products were evaluated for compliance with DOE Human Subjects Research requirements. DOE concluded that the project does not meet the definition of human subjects research under the Common Rule regulations, 10 CFR 745.

DOE compliance evaluation

The project and its research products were reviewed by DOE to determine whether the work constituted Human Subjects Research. DOE concluded that the project does not meet the definition of Human Subjects Research under the Common Rule regulations.

Central DOE IRB determination

The Central Department of Energy Institutional Review Board noted that the current research does not involve a living individual about whom an investigator obtains, uses, studies, analyzes, or generates identifiable private information or identifiable biospecimens. If the research changes, an additional consultation with the IRB would be required.

Data assurance documentation

To comply with DOE requirements, a data assurance document was completed and executed. This documentation supports the project’s compliance posture and defines the data assurance basis for the research products.

DOE Human Subjects Research determination: not Human Subjects Research under the Common Rule regulations, 10 CFR 745.

Privacy Impact Assessment

Privacy review completed for compliant data collection and AI/ML development

The PANDAWN Privacy Impact Assessment was successfully completed in coordination with ANL Legal, the DOE Site Office, and NNSA Legal. This review confirms the project’s privacy-by-design approach and clears a key regulatory milestone for data collection, curation, and AI/ML algorithm development.

No PII collection

The assessment confirms that the PANDAWN system collects no personally identifiable information. This finding was verified by the DOE Institutional Review Board through image sampling review.

Privacy-by-design camera placement

Cameras are positioned to capture high-level, bird’s-eye views of intersections only. The system is not designed to identify individuals, read license plates, or track movement over time.

Limited coordinated image collection

Image collection uses short, coordinated bursts across nodes to support scientific analysis while preventing temporal tracking of individuals. These collection practices are governed by the project privacy policy and the agreement with the City of Chicago.

Regulatory milestone completed

Completion of the Privacy Impact Assessment clears a key regulatory milestone, enabling the project to move forward with data collection and AI/ML algorithm development.
