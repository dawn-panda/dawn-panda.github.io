The PANDAWN
Sensor Network

A remotely reconfigurable, AI-enabled radiological and contextual sensor network deployed across Chicago — a city-scale testbed for networked multimodal detection.

Hardware

Node anatomy

Sensor node — components

NaI(Tl) Radiation Detector 2×4×16" crystal array · custom housing · variable angle List-mode Digibase acquisition; raw spectral data. Each node weighs ~60 lbs including the NaI crystal.
Video Camera 5 MP · 20–30 fps · motorized lens Bird's-eye intersection view. Privacy-by-design: camera angle insufficient for face or license plate recognition, verified by DOE IRB.
LiDAR 64-beam · 10 Hz · variable tilt 3D point clouds enabling vehicle tracking and depth-aware source–object attribution.
Meteorological Sensors T · p · H at /30 s · rain gauge at /1 s Continuous environmental context critical for correlating radiological background fluctuations with weather conditions.
Edge Computing 2× NVIDIA Xavier NX · 2× Raspberry Pi 4 AI/ML inference, data acquisition, ROS messaging, and built-in cellular/Wi-Fi connectivity — all on-pole.
  • Time-synchronized multimodal acquisition All sensor streams are tied together through a ROS-based DAQ system. Precise time synchronization across radiological, camera, LiDAR, and environmental data is a key feature enabling multimodal fusion algorithms.
  • Continuous + selected acquisition modes Rad and meteorological data stream continuously (~2.5 GB/node/day). Camera and LiDAR are captured 6×2 min per day, producing ~12 GB/node/day. Total: ~15 GB/node/day.
  • COTS-first, extensible design Primarily commercial off-the-shelf components with modular PoE and USB expansion. Containerized ML workloads can be pushed, swapped, or updated remotely without physical access.
  • Closed-loop edge–cloud computing Nodes operate autonomously and transmit inference results in near-real-time. The cloud pushes updated models, job configurations, and adaptive steering commands back to the edge.

History

Program timeline

2014–2019

Waggle AI@Edge & Array of Things

ANL, UChicago, and Northwestern build the Waggle edge computing platform and deploy environmental sensing nodes across Chicago in collaboration with the City.

2019–2024

PANDA (LBNL) & DAWN (ANL)

Parallel projects add radiological/nuclear sensing and detection algorithms. Rad/Nuc sensors added to Chicago city agreement in 2020.

Summer 2022

DAWN Pilot Deployment

2-node pilot deployed in Chicago Medical District. Communication plan developed with advisory group from FBI, DHS, Chicago Fire Dept., and ANL.

Summer 2023

Full 14-Node Deployment Begins

First full data campaign: 12 TB collected Sept 2023 – May 2024.

January 2024

DOE Human Subjects Research Review

DOE IRB confirms no PII is collected. Data assurance agreement executed. DOE concludes work does not meet the definition of human subjects research under 10CFR745.

Spring–Fall 2024

Operational Pause & Transition

Operations temporarily paused while DHS and NNSA resolved authority and responsibilities. Nodes and resources transferred from DAWN to the joint PANDAWN project.

Spring 2026

Privacy Impact Assessment Executed — Network Restarted

Formal PIA completed with ANL Legal, DOE Site Office, and NNSA Legal. PANDAWN network resumes operations. FY26–FY27 data campaign begins (~94 TB expected).

Privacy & Compliance

Privacy guardrails

  • No personally identifiable information collected Confirmed by DOE IRB through image sampling review. No audio, genomic, or biological data is collected.
  • Privacy-by-design camera positioning Cameras positioned for bird's-eye intersection views only; images insufficient to identify faces or read license plates.
  • Limited video collection LiDAR and video collected only 6×2 min/day (<1%). Radiological and environmental data collected continuously.
  • Data access controls Access restricted to approved team members at ANL, PNNL, and LBNL. All users must sign a Data User Agreement.
