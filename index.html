Project overview

Three integrated research thrusts

🌐

Acquire, Label & Curate

Operate the PANDAWN sensor network, apply ML methods for automatic data labeling, and disseminate curated multimodal data sets to the NA-22 research community via the Berkeley Data Cloud.

📡

Explore Multi-Sensor Data

Investigate radiological pattern-of-life, develop new detection algorithms, and integrate analysis methods directly into the live labeling and curation pipeline.

🤖

AI-Driven Domain Awareness

Explore and evaluate large transformer and multimodal AI models for radiological anomaly detection, spatial/temporal data fusion, and domain-aware interpretation of sensor data.

Impact

Why PANDAWNOps matters

Sensor Network

A unique city-scale testbed for new concepts in multimodal sensing, networked radiation detection, and edge–cloud computing — driving innovation across the nonproliferation mission space.

Multimodal Data

The community has clearly articulated the need for diverse, labeled, real-world multimodal data. PANDAWNOps provides this for algorithm development, model training, and establishing urban baselines of "normal."

Fusion Algorithms

Algorithms fusing temporal and spatial data across multiple sensor modalities increase detection sensitivity and situational awareness, with significant leverage potential across mission areas.

AI-Driven Analytics

AI methods combining accurate sensor fusion with domain-aware interpretation are essential for improved detection and decision making — a need articulated by the Genesis Mission grand challenges.

Project tasks

Scope and deliverables

  • Network Operation & Maintenance (Tasks 1.2–4.2) Maintain stable operation of the sensor network including nodes, infrastructure, and edge algorithms. Scope covers periodic maintenance and security upgrades but not major sensor or algorithm modifications. Led by ANL.
  • Data QC, Labeling & Curation Requirements (Task 1.3) Develop requirements for quality control, labeling, curation, and sharing of multimodal data, in engagement with the NA-22 community via DRAG and Project Alexandria. Joint LBNL & ANL.
  • Automatic Data Labeling & Curation Pipeline (Tasks 1.4–3.3) Develop, implement, and maintain an integrated automated pipeline to label R/N and contextual data and curate it in the Berkeley Data Cloud. Application to live data in FY26. Led by LBNL.
  • Large Transformer Models for Domain Awareness (Tasks 3.4–4.3) Explore and evaluate AI methods for radiological-contextual anomaly detection, spatial/temporal data fusion, and domain-aware interpretation. Three themes: anomaly detection, multimodal fusion, and domain-aware analysis. Joint LBNL, ANL, PNNL.
  • Adaptive Data Collection (Tasks 3.5–4.4) Demonstrate the use of historical priors and real-time inference to steer data acquisition — enabling tipping and cueing, e.g., increased video capture during anomaly periods. Leverages PANDAWN's unique edge–cloud compute capabilities. Joint LBNL & ANL.
Background

Building on a decade of urban sensing

PANDAWNOps (FY24–present) is the operational phase of the PANDAWN network, built by combining the PANDA project (LBNL, FY19–24) and the DAWN project (ANL, FY19–24) — both rooted in the Waggle AI@Edge urban sensing platform that has operated in Chicago since 2014.

The network is deployed under a multi-party agreement with the City of Chicago and the University of Illinois Chicago. A Privacy Impact Assessment was completed in Spring 2026 in coordination with ANL Legal, DOE Site Office, and NNSA Legal, confirming no personally identifiable information is collected and enabling current operations.
