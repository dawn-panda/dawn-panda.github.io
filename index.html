Project overview
Networked, multimodal sensing and associated data fusion are increasingly important across the nonproliferation mission space, particularly as AI-enabled methods become central to detection, characterization, and decision-support workflows. However, the development, validation, and testing of these methods require large volumes of synchronized, labeled, multimodal data collected under realistic operating conditions. The PANDAWN network addresses this need by enabling data collection across a wide geographic area, producing a diverse dataset that supports both multimodal fusion research and the exploration of geospatial data processing techniques. Its ability to support long-term, continuous data collection further allows environmental, meteorological, and seasonal conditions to be sampled over time, enabling the evaluation of temporal and spatial trends in ways that were previously not possible. Together, these capabilities provide a unique real-world dataset shaped by complex operational and environmental variables that are difficult to accurately reproduce through simulation alone. This makes PANDAWN a valuable platform for advancing AI model development, algorithm testing, and data fusion approaches relevant to nonproliferation applications.
Project Goals

Advancing multimodal sensing, data curation, and AI-enabled domain awareness

The project builds on the PANDAWN network to collect, curate, and analyze real-world multimodal sensing data across geographically distributed sites. These efforts support data-driven algorithm development, automated labeling, and AI-enabled analysis for nonproliferation-relevant applications.

Acquire, label, and curate PANDAWN multi-sensor data

  • Operate the PANDAWN network for sustained multimodal data collection.
  • Disseminate curated datasets to the NA-22 community.
  • Apply machine learning methods for automatic data labeling.
  • Integrate data science methods into acquisition and curation workflows.

Explore multi-sensor data and radiological pattern-of-life

  • Analyze radiological pattern-of-life across time, location, and environmental conditions.
  • Develop new algorithms for multi-sensor data interpretation.
  • Incorporate validated analysis methods into the labeling and curation pipeline.

Evaluate AI methods for multimodal analysis and domain awareness

  • Assess AI techniques for fusing heterogeneous sensing modalities.
  • Evaluate model performance using realistic, geographically distributed data.
  • Advance domain-awareness capabilities for complex real-world environments.
